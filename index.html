Research Paper

The Chatbot Knows It's You: Dialogue Attribution in Unauthenticated Human-LLM Sessions

2025 - 2026

Problem: Investigated whether the same user can be re-identified across anonymous Human-LLM sessions, even when topic, time, and domain change.

Data: Built and evaluated on WildAuth, a benchmark derived from real-world ChatGPT logs with open-set user splits, up to 442 users, 14 coarse topics, and up to 18,630 dialogue pairs across easy, medium, and hard settings.

Approach: Proposed UMA, an uncertainty-aware multi-aspect attribution framework that fuses personality, interaction dynamics, stylometrics, and semantic content to recover stable identity signals under topic drift and short-text noise.

Outcome / Impact: Achieved up to 92.05% AUC and 76.15% F1 in the challenging cross-topic setting. Also showed that LLM responses alone remain linkable at 88.65% AUC, reframing "anonymous AI use" as a measurable privacy risk.

My Contribution: Co-first author. Contributed to problem framing, benchmark design logic, multi-aspect modeling narrative, experiment analysis, and technical writing.

Research Design / Experimental Study

Personality Elicitation via LLM Communication Strategies

2026

Problem: Asked whether latent Big Five traits can be actively elicited from short, task-oriented Human-LLM interactions, where users usually provide only sparse personality cues.

Data: Designed a dual evaluation pipeline with LLM-based simulation and a human-subject study plan. The simulated setting covered diverse Big Five combinations under a strict 10-turn conversation budget with IPIP-NEO style ground truth.

Approach: Built an adaptive closed-loop framework grounded in Trait Activation Theory and Nudging Theory. The method mapped dialogue situations to activatable traits, then embedded IPIP-inspired scenarios into assistant communication strategies.

Outcome / Impact: Active elicitation outperformed the baseline overall, lifting average accuracy from 0.67 to 0.74 and average F1 from 0.62 to 0.72. The analysis also exposed a drop on Openness, guiding the next step toward real-time profiling and targeted trait selection.

My Contribution: Designed the research logic, evaluation setup, presentation narrative, and the translation from psychological theory into a practical LLM interaction framework.
